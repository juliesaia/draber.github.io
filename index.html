Spelling Bee Assistant is an add-on for Spelling Bee, the New York Times’ popular word puzzle. It’s packed with features that will boost your playing experience.

Follow your progress, see what lies ahead of you and if you want to, get a hint or two. Eventually, when you run out of ideas, reveal today’s answers - no more waiting until the next day!

Spelling Bee Assistant is a great improvement for this excellent game and it won’t spoil the fun. Promised!

What’s new in SBA 4.2.1?

While version 4 brought better mobile compatibility and a configurable Dark Mode, version 4.2 puts a spotlight on the Spelling Bee community. Some fellow players had reached out asking to add links to the different forums and to highlight some of the non-official features. Well, here they are!

4.2.1 is a bug fix release that addresses some minor issues.

Feature Overview

  • The main panel

    These tools have been part of the Spelling Bee Assistant for a long while, albeit under different names or in other forms.

    They all work in the same fashion; they show you how many words you’ve found so far (✓), how many are still in the pot (?) and the sum thereof (∑). In addition, Score displays the same information for points.

    Score represents your all-over progress. Letter count and First letter group the words by their length, respectively, their first letter. The row containing the center letter is highlighted. Pangrams used to be part of Letter count but now have their own block.

    Once a line has been completed, it will be greyed out.

  • The Assistant menu

    Show Assistant allows you to show or hide the main panel. Score, Letter count, First letter and Pangrams let you configure which tools it contains.

    The Progress Bar has its home at the top of the result list and Spill the beans appears above the hive.

    Dark Mode unsurprisingly applies a dark color scheme to the game and Dark Mode Colors lets you choose your favorite colors for the said scheme.

    Grid, Your Progress and Today’s Answers are displayed as modal pop-ups and Spelling Bee Assistant eventually is a link to this website.

  • The progress bar displays your advancement based on your points so far. Clicking on it reveals the more detailed Your Progress screen.

  • With every letter you type, this emoticon reveals whether you’re on the right track or barking up the wrong tree.

  • Many users prefer darker interfaces, especially on larger screens. Spelling Bee Assistant had Dark Mode support from the beginning on, but now it’s even better because…

  • …SBA 4 introduces Dark Mode configuration. It offers a range of 12 different color schemes and lets you pick your favorite one. Why having just one Dark Mode when you can have a dozen?

  • An overview of the different ranks and how far you made it into your journey. You can also launch the Your Progress screen by clicking on the Progress Bar.

  • Letter count and First letter from the main panel give you a brief overview of the words that you’ve found and those that remain. The Grid screen combines these two views into a handy matrix.

  • Bingo, Perfect Pangram, GN4L - confused yet? The NYT has a glossary of common terms people use when talking about the Spelling Bee. Some terms refer to game features and some are addidtional fun challenges.

    So, where is this community? Well, there is more then just one way to join fellow players and the Assistant will you show you how to find them.

  • When you’re eventually out of words but are dying to know the solution, the Today’s Answers screen will satisfy your curiosity!

    All words are linked to the respective terms in Google; maybe this will help you next time!

    Spelling Bee’s Yesterday’s Answers has also been enriched with Google links.

  • Spelling Bee Assistant uses responsive design and has been tested on many screen sizes. If the system requirements are met, there is a good chance it will work on your mobile device.

    I would want to make a bolder statement, but in reality, mobile screens and browsers are so different that it’s hard to predict whether the installation will succeed. Let me know how it went!

System requirements

Spelling Bee Assistant works with modern browsers and has originally been built with laptops or desktop computers in mind. The extension works in the Kiwi browser under Android. According to user reports, the bookmarklet can be used on Safari/iPhone instead. The user experience on mobile devices might be limited due to the screen size.

Installing the extension on Kiwi (Android only)

  1. Install Kiwi from the Google Play Store
  2. Open it and visit Spelling Bee Assistant’s Chrome Store page
  3. Click on Add to Chrome
  4. Click on OK on the message that appears

Kiwi will likely display an error message upon installation, but - at least in my experience - it will work regardless.

Installing the bookmarklet on mobile devices

Doesn’t it spoil the game?

There are numerous websites with a similar purpose, which - according to this article - the Spelling Bee community seems to enjoy. The challenge stays the same and so does the fun. But now you have a little helper at hand when you’re stuck in a corner.

What is a bookmarklet?

Bookmarklets are small pieces of code that can enhance the functionality of a website. They reside, just like regular bookmarks, in your browser’s bookmark menu. Unfortunately, there is no straightforward way to assign an icon to them.

Your privacy

This site doesn’t use any technology to collect personally identifiable information about you. It is, along with the bookmarklet, hosted on GitHub Pages. Please refer to their statement regarding the data they collect. I don’t have access to this data.

Depending on the store, installing the extension will display a message that reads "This extension can read and change your data on www.nytimes.com [...]", or similar. Understandably, this may raise some concerns. In fact, the extension reads Spelling Bee’s game data, nothing else. It doesn’t access, modify or submit any personal data whatsoever.

Most add-on stores provide download statistics, sometimes grouped by country, but never any personally identifiable information.
